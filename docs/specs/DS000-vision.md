# DS000 - Vision

## Introduction
The project starts from a methodological problem rather than from a preferred model family. Ruliology studies what abstract rules do and how spaces of possible rules are organized [WOLFRAM-2026]. Applied category theory studies disciplined composition and structure-preserving translation across domains [FONG-SPIVAK-2019]. Neuro-symbolic work argues for typed and compositional concept structures that can be represented jointly through symbolic and learned components [MAO-2025]. These lines can reinforce each other, but only if the architecture respects a basic distinction: a text report is not the phenomenon itself, and it is not yet a theory of that phenomenon.

The vision therefore begins with two constraints. The first is structural: internal organization must not be erased too early. Reports, traces, and observations often carry entities, roles, temporal order, admissible updates, and partially stable invariants. A useful induction system must preserve that structure long enough for it to shape the theories that are later proposed. The second constraint is meta-rational: finite evidence rarely determines one uniquely privileged theory. Several local theories may remain plausible at different granularities or under different notions of invariance, composition, and observer access.

## Core Content
The objective is not to infer a final theory directly from text. The objective is to construct, under explicit uncertainty, a bounded local region of theory space around the available evidence. The system first lifts an input text `x` into a family of observational hypotheses `O(x) = {O1, ..., On}`. Each hypothesis records what may have been observed, what was stated explicitly, what was only weakly inferred, and what remains unresolved. A hypothesis is already structured, but it is not yet a theory. This makes the library an engine for disciplined compression under uncertainty: compression is valuable only when it preserves observational support, typed structure, and explicit uncertainty rather than hiding them behind one premature answer.

From each observational hypothesis, the system induces candidate local theories `T = (Sigma, I, R, C, S)`. A local theory contains a frame-like state schema, empirically supported invariants, rewrite templates, a discipline of admissible composition, and a decomposed score profile. Several such theories may remain compatible with the same observational hypothesis. The system therefore does not stop at candidate generation. It organizes compatible theories into a local neighborhood `N(Oi) = (Ti, Mi, Ei, Qi)`, where `Mi` records typed transformations such as refinement, coarsening, refactorization, and observer shift, `Ei` records observer-relative equivalence classes, and `Qi` records robust properties that remain stable across the retained neighborhood.

Meta-rationality is the retention discipline that governs this neighborhood. It does not mean vagueness and it does not mean that every theory should be kept forever. It means that the system maintains a bounded frontier of structured explanatory candidates for as long as the evidence, the task, or the cost model does not justify stronger commitment. The frontier is therefore the operational form of epistemic restraint. It preserves plural structure where pluralism is warranted, and it makes the reasons for subsequent preference visible.

This vision also defines the role of `autoResearchLib`. The repository is the reference implementation of the framework, not the framework itself. The library provides reusable machinery for observational lifting, local theory induction, local neighborhood construction, discriminating questions, frontier update, and canonical serialization of the resulting frontier state. The current domain bundles `package`, `sample`, and `manuscript` are validation scaffolds rather than built-in limits. The deeper claim is that the same architecture should transfer to logs, traces, extracted event chains, protocol evidence, scientific workflows, and other structured sources of local regularity.

The current experiments validate three pieces of this broader vision. Richer observers reduce frontier entropy earlier than coarse observers, explicit discriminating questions shrink the active frontier without forcing opaque collapse, and retained frontiers preserve recoverability when decisive cues are masked. Those experiments do not yet exhaust the framework. They demonstrate that the architectural discipline has empirical traction in a controlled setting and that the resulting objects can be serialized, inspected, and discussed without hand-waving.

## Conclusion
The project should therefore be understood as a serious and bounded research implementation. Its contribution is not a claim to universal theory induction. Its contribution is a disciplined architecture for moving from reports to observational hypotheses, from hypotheses to local theories, and from local theories to a bounded, transformable, and auditable neighborhood of plausible explanations.

### Critical Implementation Directives
1. Keep the distinction between textual report, observational hypothesis, and local theory explicit in both code and documentation.
2. Preserve several plausible theories through a bounded frontier and make the reasons for retention visible.
3. Treat typed transformations, observer-relative equivalence, discriminating questions, frontier update, and canonical usage traces as core behavior rather than optional commentary.
